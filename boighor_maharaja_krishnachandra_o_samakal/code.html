Synopsis

Maharaja Krishnachandra o Samakal is a seminal research-driven anthology that explores the vibrant, complex, and transformative era of 18th-century Bengal under the reign of Maharaja Krishnachandra Ray of Nadia. Often remembered as a patron of literature and a pivotal political figure during the Battle of Plassey, Krishnachandra’s influence stretched far beyond the borders of his kingdom.

This volume, meticulously curated by Soibal Sarkar, Tamal Bandhyopadhya, and Anirban Jana, bridges the gap between historical scholarship and narrative accessibility. It delves into the cultural renaissance of the period, the development of the Bengali language, and the intricate social hierarchies that defined the "Samakal" or contemporary times.
